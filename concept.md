## Slide 2 — The Problem
### 450 million workers. Zero of them can use Apna Jobs.

**No smartphone literacy**
~40% of blue-collar workers cannot navigate touchscreen job apps — too complex, too English-centric.

**Exploitative middlemen**
Workers rely on contractors (*thekedar*) who take commissions of ₹500–₹2,000 per placement and offer no transparency.

**Geography locked**
Labour nakas are localised. A mason in Yelahanka can't access construction jobs in Whitefield, 20 km away.

**Employers pay more**
Without a reliable channel, employers lose 2–4 weeks finding verified workers, costing ₹15,000+ per unfilled role.

> **Speaker notes:** The core insight: the interface is the barrier, not the market. Every incumbent assumes smartphone literacy. We don't.

## Slide 3 — The Solution
### Works on any ₹500 feature phone.

**Call flow:**
1. Worker calls toll-free number *(any phone)*
2. Speaks skill & location *(in Hindi / Kannada / Telugu)*
3. AI matches jobs instantly *(STT → LLM → DB)*
4. Jobs read aloud *(top 3 matches)*
5. Confirmed & connected *(SMS + employer alert)*

**No internet. No literacy required. Works on any phone.**

> **Speaker notes:** Walk through the call flow. Emphasise: a migrant construction worker in Peenya with a ₹500 Nokia can now access the full job market.

## Slide 7 — Business Model
### Employers pay. Workers are always free.

| Revenue stream | Price | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Employer subscription | ₹999–₹4,999/mo | Unlimited job postings + verified worker leads. Tiered by listing volume and lead quality. |
| Per-placement fee | ₹150–₹500/hire | Charged on confirmed hires. Tracked via callback or employer verification. |
| B2G data contracts | ₹5L–₹25L/yr | Skill-gap data and job-matching infra licensed to state labour depts and NSDC. |

**Unit economics at 30 employers + 500 placements/month:**
- Revenue: ₹8.9L/month
- IVR cost: ₹1.5L
- LLM + infra: ₹0.5L
- Net (pre-fixed): ₹6.9L
- Contribution margin: ~78%

Variable cost per call: ₹1.50 | Revenue per call: ₹8.90 | Breakeven: ~22 paid employers

> **Speaker notes:** Emphasise: workers are permanently free. This is a B2B2C model — employers fund the flywheel.

## Slide 9 — Go-to-Market
### Bengaluru first. Employer supply → worker demand.

**Phase 1 (M1–M3): Employer seeding**
- Direct sales to 20 construction / logistics firms
- Enterprise accounts (L&T, Porter, Swiggy vendors)
- IVR live in Kannada, Hindi, Telugu
- *Target: 20 employers · 200+ listings*

**Phase 2 (M4–M7): Worker demand activation**
- Auto-rickshaw stickers + FM radio (Kannada/Hindi)
- Contractor agent network (₹200/placement incentive)
- Karnataka Labour Dept pilot (PM Vishwakarma alignment)
- *Target: 5K calls/mo · ₹2L MRR*

**Phase 3 (M8–M12): Scale + city 2**
- 500 test worker calls via labour nakas
- Convert pilot employers to ₹1,999/mo plan
- Hyderabad launch (Telugu already live)
- *Target: 30K calls/mo · ₹12L MRR*

> **Speaker notes:** Employer supply must precede worker marketing. Empty IVR experience kills word-of-mouth. This sequencing is deliberate.
